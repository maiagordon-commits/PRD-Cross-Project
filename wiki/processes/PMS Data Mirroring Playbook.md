# PMS Data Mirroring Playbook
> This playbook structures the workshop output for improving how old PMS data is mirrored into Guesty for accurate financial data.

## Summary
This playbook is based on two workshops with Product, Financials, Distribution, and Implementation stakeholders. The goal is to define a detailed per-channel operating view for the current PMS data-mirroring pain points, existing solutions, potential solutions, and assumptions.

The Fellow links have been preserved as source evidence, but the actual meeting-note content was not accessible in this environment. The page therefore captures the confirmed workshop context and provides the durable structure needed to synthesize the notes once Maia shares accessible content or pasted summaries.

- What is the discussed use case?
- What is the current pain point?
- What does each channel currently have related to this pain point?
- What is the current solution?
- What is the potential solution?
- What assumptions must be validated?

## Cross-Channel Questions
- Which fields are required across all channels for accurate financial data?
- Which fields are channel-specific?
- Which parts of old PMS data can be reliably mirrored, and which need manual validation?
- Who owns data validation before go-live or financial reconciliation?
- What can be standardized versus handled as channel-specific exceptions?
- Which gaps require product changes versus implementation playbook changes?

## Risks / Blockers to Watch
- Old PMS data may be incomplete, inconsistent, or unavailable.
- Channel-specific data models may prevent a single standardized mirroring process.
- Manual workarounds may not scale across enterprise implementations.
- Financial accuracy may depend on validation steps owned by multiple teams.
- Without clear ownership, issue resolution may bounce between Implementation, Distribution, and Financials.
