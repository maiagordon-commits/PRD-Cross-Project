Project Schedule and Coordination: Rinat Elimelech and Maia Gordon confirmed that the next release is scheduled for July 10th. The team discussed the "moving the cheese" process, which Maia Gordon intends to initiate via a communication thread following the call, with Rinat Elimelech requesting that the team add their comments to that thread for alignment (00:00:26).
Staffing Changes: The team acknowledged that Alex Borack will be departing at the end of the month. Alex Borack noted they were the primary gatekeeper for onboarding and expressed confidence that the team would continue to manage the workload effectively despite the transition (00:02:42).
Booking.com Bundle Fees and Beast Tool Issues: Alex Borack reported that 2,000 reservations require migration to Deduct 2.0 to adjust fees and carry forward owner payments for the May statement period. However, the Beast tool is failing to migrate these reservations because they are in a locked accounting period. Additionally, Alex Borack identified a bug in the Beast tool where it duplicates zero-dollar fees during migration (00:04:39).
Proposed AI Agent for Data Mapping: Alex Borack proposed an initiative to use an AI agent to handle CSV conversions and automate data mapping for onboarding. The goal is to feed the agent file definitions to reduce manual calculation time. Ella Waldman and Maia Gordon agreed to explore this solution, with Alex Borack planning to involve team leads to assist in training the agent, utilizing experience from past projects like Streamline and Vicasa (00:07:55).
Product Strategy for Reservation Imports: The team discussed the "source of truth" for reservations. Ella Waldman and Alex Borack proposed moving away from relying solely on channel-driven adjustments. Instead, they suggested a tool that identifies mapping patterns during import and creates "settings snapshots" directly on the reservation. This would allow the system to treat these as official settings rather than temporary adjustments, reducing ongoing overhead (00:12:50).
Operational Impact of Settings-Based Overrides: Alex Borack estimated that implementing a settings-based approach instead of manual adjustments could reduce the time required to manage Avari from five or six hours per month down to approximately 30 minutes, once the AI tool is available. The team agreed this would provide a more consistent source of truth than relying on channel overrides (00:18:21).
Troubleshooting Migration Blockers: Javier Ibarz joined the call to assist with the Beast tool migration failures for March and April reservations. Javier Ibarz clarified that the system does not allow migrations in locked accounting periods. Alex Borack confirmed that approximately 1,200 reservations needed to be processed before June 1st to ensure accurate owner statements (00:21:08).
Escalation to Tier 3 Support: Due to the limitations of the Beast tool with locked periods, the team agreed to attempt a different path. Rinat Elimelech and Rotem Meir suggested escalating the task to Tier 3 support to handle the adjustments via the API. Alex Borack agreed to create a ticket by the end of the next day to facilitate this, while continuing to check if the Beast tool could be used for smaller batches (00:25:35).
VRBO Data Quality Challenges: The team discussed recurring issues with data quality from VRBO, specifically regarding zero-dollar fees appearing on invoices. Javier Ibarz explained that this is a known issue stemming from channel managers sending incorrect data, and noted that it is currently being tracked in the VRBO backlog (00:29:01).
Strategic Decisions on Legacy Fees: Alex Borack and Ella Waldman debated whether to zero out all legacy fees from channels or retain them. They concluded that while keeping channel data provides consistency, the best long-term strategy is to fix adjustments as settings to prevent them from being overridden. This approach avoids "hacks" and keeps the system stable, even if the initial imported data appears messy (00:31:27).
Handling Tax Configurations and Management Fees: Alex Borack raised questions about how the proposed settings-based solution would handle management fees that are subject to tax, especially when those fees might be configured differently across regions. Ella Waldman explained that tax calculations are the final step in the process and that the proposed method should allow for distinct tax treatment on these fees (00:36:01).
Australia Tax Example Analysis: The team examined a specific Australian use case (Hometown/Airbnb) to understand the complexity of fee and tax imports. Ella Waldman and Javier Ibarz noted that in Australia, fees were often bundled in legacy systems and that GST (Goods and Services Tax) is applied at 10% to fees while rent is often non-taxable, creating discrepancies in how reservations are imported (00:39:54).
Tax Eligibility and Listings: Javier Ibarz clarified that the ability to push taxes to Airbnb depends on whether the property manager’s listing is eligible to define taxes. In Australia, listings are often ineligible, meaning the system receives gross values, which complicates the tax breakout process (00:44:04).
Investigation of Reservation Logs: The team reviewed reservation logs for a specific property to identify why resort and cleaning fees were appearing. They determined that the fees were imported from the legacy system rather than stemming from current Guest settings. Javier Ibarz verified that the fees were present in the reservation snapshot from the channel (00:47:12).
Conclusion on International Standardization: The group concluded that because tax and fee handling varies significantly by region, there is no universal "one size fits all" solution for importing reservations. They recognized that while the proposed AI-driven settings approach would simplify onboarding and maintenance, they must continue to address regional variations in channel configurations (00:51:44).
Import Mapping Rules: Ella Waldman proposed a new approach for import mappings, specifically for channels like Booking.com, to define rules that handle tax items or VAT as part of the accommodation fare. The goal is to allow customers to set these preferences as part of the import process, creating a standard setting rather than a one-time adjustment.

Workshop Planning and Mapping Strategy: Maia Gordon and Alex Borack discussed the next steps for a workshop, which will include the introduction of Matt Palasio to the team. Rates and Availability Feature and Legal Risks: Javier Ibarz explained that the "Rates and Availability" feature is necessary for property managers who handle complexes with multiple room types, as content cannot be synchronized at the complex level in an Online Travel Agency setting. The current issue involves bundled fees, which risk double-charging customers on Online Travel Agencies. The team decided they must remove related tooltips, update the UI, and release a feature toggle. Because this change could lead to requests for compensation from existing customers who were unaware of the fee structures, the team will consult with legal counsel and generate a report to identify the specific accounts impacted before proceeding.
